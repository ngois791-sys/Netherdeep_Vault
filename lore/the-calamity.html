The Calamity & the Apotheon

The war that broke the world, and the mortal hero whose fall left a wound in the deep.

  • Category History
  • Era The Age of Arcanum

History The Netherdeep

At the height of the Age of Arcanum, the Betrayer Gods slipped their bonds and turned the mortal world into the battlefield of a divine war. Cities that had floated for centuries fell from the sky. Whole nations were swallowed in a single night. This is the age mortals remember only as the Calamity.

Alyxian, Beloved of the Gods

Into that age was born Alyxian: a mortal man marked from birth by the favor of the gods, a healer and a champion who carried his people through horror after horror. Where he walked, the wounded rose and the despairing took heart. For a time, it was enough.

But the Calamity was not a war that mortals could win, and one by one the people Alyxian loved were taken from him. His city fell. His prayers went unanswered. And in the silence after, with the faith of the dying still burning in him, Alyxian made a terrible choice: he took that faith for his own, and he ascended.

The Fall of the Apotheon

He became the Apotheon — a mortal raised to the threshold of godhood by grief and pride. But a power born of stolen faith could not be allowed to stand. The gods struck him down and cast him into the deepest trench of the Lucidian Ocean, and there they bound him, neither dead nor free, in the lightless cold that mortals would one day name the Netherdeep.

"He asked the gods to save his people. When they did not, he tried to become a god who would. That is the whole of the tragedy."

The Calamity ended at last, and the gods withdrew behind the Divine Gate in the event called the Divergence. But Alyxian did not die. For an age he has hung in the dark, dreaming, his sorrow leaking upward into the world — and now, faintly, that sorrow has begun to call.

Who answers that call — and what they bring back up with them — is the question our campaign exists to ask.
