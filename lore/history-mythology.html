Xhorhas History & Mythology

How the world was made and nearly unmade — the Creator gods, the Calamity, the Divergence, and the divine powers the party keeps stumbling across.

  • Category History & Myth
  • Region Exandria & Xhorhas

Before the Calamity — the Age of Arcanum

Many, many centuries ago, life was first introduced to Exandria by the Creator Deities — incredibly powerful divine beings who brought all of the components of humanity and life to the world of Exandria.

After many generations of life evolved and grew, some of these Creator Deities eventually came to resent the world that they had created, and that resentment quickly grew into hatred. These corrupted deities began to influence the land and its people, fanning the spreading flames of chaos, darkness, and hate throughout the land. These evil deities became known as the Betrayer Gods, as they betrayed the very life that they helped to create.

The other Creators, however, still loved and cherished their creation, and continually championed the opposing tenets of order, light, and life. These guardians were called the Prime Deities, and they faithfully defended their creation from the hatred and chaos of the Betrayers. As the battles worsened and the evil of the land grew stronger, the Prime Deities chose to gift the people of the world great arcane magic so that their mortal worshippers could better protect themselves from the dark power of the Betrayer Gods. These gifts took many forms, such as celestial champions, great personal boons, or powerful magical items and weapons.

With these gifts, the faithful mortals and the Prime Deities were able to fight back and ultimately banish the Betrayer Gods from the material plane, sending them to planar prisons and removing their evil influence from Exandria.

This led to the Age of Arcanum: a time where the gods stood side-by-side with mortals, and their powerful raw divine power allowed these mortals to eventually rival the power of the gods. And for a short period of time, there was peace in Exandria.

The Calamity

The peace of the Age of Arcanum was short-lived, however. As mortal powers grew and divine influence spread, evil began to take hold once more in the world. Faithful worshippers of the banished Betrayer Gods eventually gained the power and knowledge necessary to free the Betrayers from their interplanar prisons.

Returning once again to the material plane, now with even greater depths of hatred and anger, the Betrayer Gods renewed their corruption and destruction with a vengeance. The Prime Deities fought back with equal fury, and this intense clash of powers led to a centuries-long, world-shaking war, which history books called the Great War, or the Calamity.

The Divergence

The Calamity raged and devastated Exandria for many centuries. Eventually, the war ended when the Prime Deities banded together and locked their evil brethren behind a much more powerful celestial barrier, the Divine Gate.

However, even after winning the war, the Prime Deities realized that the great power that they granted to their mortal followers played a part in the war. To prevent any more divine wars from wounding Exandria again, they sorrowfully chose to leave the Material plane along with the Betrayers. They locked all deities (Prime and Betrayer alike) behind a powerful Divine Gate, forever severing the direct connection between the material plane and the divine realms.

Faithful followers and powerful allies of these deities can still hear their whispers, and many may still harness threads and echoes of their divine power. But no deity can ever step foot within Exandria again. This event came to be known as the Divergence.

After the Calamity — the Era of Reclamation

The war was ultimately won by the Prime Deities, and their creation was saved from total destruction. But the war was won at a great cost. The lands throughout Exandria are now deeply battle-scarred, especially in areas where historically fierce battles took place, such as the region of Xhorhas. Many demons and other supernatural beasts set free during the war still roam to this day. A terribly small portion of the population of Exandria survived, and the world was left to rebuild and rule itself alone, with no divine powers to directly interfere (for good or for evil).

The Betrayer Gods

The nine Betrayer Gods are powerful, evil deities that fought against the Prime Deities, seeking to corrupt and destroy the very life in Exandria that many of them helped to build. After being banished from the Material plane for several centuries, these evil gods were eventually released from their interdimensional prisons with renewed fury and vengeance. Their release, and the war that ensued, led directly to the events of the Calamity and the Divergence.

Faithful fanatics of these evil beings still inhabit Exandria, but they must worship in secrecy. Open worship of the Betrayers is strictly prohibited in many regions of Exandria, including within the Kryn Dynasty.

The Prime Deities

The Prime Deities were the gods that fought to defend and protect Exandria from the Betrayers. They gifted the people of Exandria with divine magic so that they could better defend themselves against the legions of evil.

When the Betrayer Gods were finally banished during the Divergence, the Prime Deities sadly chose to remove themselves from the Material plane as well, believing that their continued presence on the plane would ultimately cause more harm than good. They willingly left Exandria, and sealed themselves away behind the Divine Gate along with their Betrayer brethren, never again to set foot in the material plane.

Open worship of the Prime Deities is allowed within the Kryn Dynasty, but is often considered a bit superstitious and “old-fashioned.” The majority of the citizens of the Kryn Dynasty are followers of the Luxon.

Vestiges of Divergence

Vestiges of Divergence are a class of ancient and powerful artifacts, imbued with divine power, that were created and/or used during the events of the Calamity. Since the Calamity, however, these items have been scattered around the world. They are very rare, very powerful, and very valuable.

The golden pendant that was found in the Emerald Grotto (referred to as a “Jewel” by the man in your vision) is very likely to be one of these vestiges, a historical item referred to as the Jewel of Three Prayers.

Exandria’s Twin Moons

For as long as history can remember, Exandria has had two moons. Catha is a large, beautiful white moon, which follows a predictable orbit and has regular phases. This moon is typically associated with the goddess Sehanine, and is a symbol of both the followers of Sehanine and many other sea-based cultures.

Ruidus is the smaller, more distant of the two moons — about half the size of its twin, Catha. Glowing a deep red, this moon doesn’t have phases, and is always full whenever it appears. It has a very complicated, seemingly chaotic orbit, and doesn’t follow predictable paths the same way that Catha does.

Ruidus typically appears along with its twin, but very rarely it can rise as the only moon in the sky when Catha has gone dark. This event is referred to as a “flare.” The more superstitious Exandrians consider Ruidus to be a symbol of bad luck, and the rare Ruidus flare is an even darker and more ominous omen of ill fortune.
