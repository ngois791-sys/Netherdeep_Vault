Visions & Dreams

Everything the party has glimpsed in vision and dream — the drowning warrior, his winged champion, and the curse of the red moon.

Vision from session #4, immediately after Ayo grabbed the glowing, golden pendant off of the pedestal during the Emerald Grotto race:

The light around the pedestal fades as a golden, spectral figure in the form of a male human rises from the amulet. He has the appearance of a young, but seasoned warrior. He is dressed in leather armor and a tattered red cape, and holds a shield and a banged-up bronze helmet in his hands. His face is framed by long curly hair, and he looks straight at you, imploringly, with large, dark eyes that hold a deep sadness. He reaches out a spectral hand imploringly, and speaks to you: “I am imprisoned, betrayed, lost. Please, help me.”

Suddenly, the ground vanishes beneath your feet, and you fall, tumbling through a vortex of golden light. You fall deeper, deeper, deeper, then suddenly stop. The golden light abruptly disappears, and you find yourself suspended in a pitch-black expanse. You feel water buoying you. An intense darkness, coldness, and pressure wraps around you claustrophobically. You can't even see your hands in front of you, and movement feels almost impossible due to the crushing weight and cold around you. Just before panic starts to set in, a dim, eerie red light appears in the distance. As you move toward it, slowly, painfully, barely feeling as if you're making any progress, you get close enough to see the red light is illuminating the same warrior you saw before.

He is shackled to the ground from his wrists, neck, and ankles by undulating black tendrils appearing like thick chains. Upon looking closer, they are pulsating and undulating like a living thing, and are shot through with sickly-looking red glowing veins.

The warrior’s appearance is much more solid than the spectral image from before, but instead of the proud glowing golden warrior from before, here he slumps against his shackles, head bowed. Defeated, exhausted, hopeless. You hear him murmuring to himself, quietly and repeatedly in a tired voice: “Moon Weaver, I beg of you. Guide those with the power to save me to the site where I first prayed to you.” Repeating over and over, mindlessly, like a mantra that has been repeated for a very long time.

If you approach, he starts and raises his head. He looks at you with gaunt, exhausted eyes, and stares in shock, as if not sure if he can trust his eyes.

He straightens up with sudden frenzied energy:

“By the gods. Can this be real? I have been here so long and my eyes have betrayed me before."

He looks at you closer, intently, and starts speaking quickly, almost babbling in his haste to share with you: "Please. If you are real. If she has answered my prayer, oh gods if this is real and she has answered my prayer, please listen. I do not know if we have much time.

Long ago, I prayed to the Change Bringer, in the heart of a temple of great evil, and then found myself lost, imprisoned, so very alone in darkness, this never-ending darkness and cold. I beg of you, if you can truly hear me, take my jewel, defeat a moon-touched beast and receive the Moonweaver's blessing so that you may wield it, and the jewel may help you to…”

You feel consciousness leaving you, as if the pressure of the water were crushing the life out of you. Sensing this change, his voice, increasing in speed and desperation, is the last thing you hear, calling out “Please! The jewel will guide you. My name is Alyxian, and I am trapped. Remember me. Save me. Please…” ”

Dreams from session #9, experienced during a stormy night on the road to Bazzoxan, immediately after a tough battle with demons:

Torden: Your dreams have you participating in countless, unending battles. Flashes of demons and humanoids, clashing in chaotic bloody battle. Adrenaline and terror course through your body, as you find yourself cutting and slashing, drenched in black demon ichor. However, this is no glorious honor, there is none of the usual thrill to battle. You cut and slash mindlessly and endlessly, and simply try to stay alive. You have a horrible feeling that there is no winning this battle, no matter how hard you fight, and shudder in terror of what could happen if you fail. You awaken with the lingering feelings of despair and hopelessness.

Ody: Your dreams are tormented by the feeling of drowning. Intense water pressure crushes you, darkness surrounds you, cold seeps into your muscles and bones. You try to swim, but your arms and legs can barely move under the intense pressure and cold. Your own body barely obeying you, your lungs running out of air, you reach out to try and find your companions, but realize that there is no one else with you. There is nothing around you to hold on to. You are sinking, alone, in these depths, helpless, forsaken, and alone.

Sirius: On a great battlefield, you catch brief glimpses of an unknown warrior. You cannot get close enough to see them clearly, but from a distance you see the flash of light off of metal armor, a massive swinging weapon, and blindingly white feathered wings extending from their back. You watch them, locked in an intense battle with a huge demonic creature. You watch as the warrior lands multiple impressive hits on the demon, each blow creating a radiant flash of light. But then you watch in growing horror as the demon lands a blow that sends the warrior reeling and weak. The demon then pierces the warrior through with a bloody sword, you scream a soundless scream, and your mind floods with grief and sorrow.

Phoebe: You find yourself standing alone in the stone ruins of some unidentifiable ancient site. It is a foggy, silent night, and the bright light of the twin moons, red Ruidius and white Catha, shine down on you together, tinting everything around you a familiar pinkish hue. You feel a strange sensation on your arms and hands, an itching and burning that makes you look downward. You see that the veins of your hands and lower arms have become shot-through with a dark, sickly red, like a spreading infection. You can feel it pulsating through your veins, and to your horror, you can feel this strange sickness moving through the rest of your body. It begins weaving reddish threads through your mind, quiet and sinister, and you feel your control over your own thoughts and feelings start to fade and slip away, and you are left only with sudden, chaotic feelings of hostility, resentment, and hate.

Vision experienced in session #12, in the Temple of Avandra in Bazzoxan after Sirius approached a stained-glass window depicting the goddess Avandra:

"You open your eyes and find yourselves in a well-maintained garden at nighttime. The stars are shining above, along with the two twin moons. The night is warm, but a gentle breeze blows through the garden. A single torch just barely illuminates a stained-glass window, high above the garden in a stone wall. As you look down at the torch, you see a young human man sitting in the grass before the stained glass. His head is bowed, thick brown curls of hair are plastered to his brow by sweat. He has fallen to his knees in a posture of desperation, and leans wearily on a well-worn longsword. His eyes betray a lack of sleep, and despite his youth, deep lines and shadows in his face show that he has experienced more pain than any one person should bear."

“I’m exhausted, and I am frightened.” “They’re counting on me, and I must lead them. But this battle seems impossible. Moon Weaver has lent her champion and her blessing, but can one deity stand against such evil? What more can I do? How many more will be lost? How much more can I bear?"

His voice chokes up, and he bows his head even deeper. "Please. Someone must tell me—is our story already decided, or may it yet be rewritten?"

The moons race across the sky and time passes, as he remains in the garden, sleeplessly, head bowed, desperately murmuring to himself. The moons are eventually replaced by the angry red sun that rises before a fateful battle. A small group of silhouettes approach the temple, and one figure breaks away and slowly approaches the kneeling man. You catch a glimpse of large white wings extending from the approaching figure's back as they kneel down and embrace him.

You see them murmur together for several long moments, their voices muffled and incoherent as if traveling through water. After a bit, they press their heads together comfortingly, and then stand and clasp hands in some type of warrior's salute. The young man walks over to the other group of figures, and they all exchange clasped hands, comforting embraces, and more somber words. As the group turns away to leave the temple, a beam of light reaches through the stained-glass depiction of Avandra and glints off of a golden pendant hanging from the young man's neck. "

Visions experienced during session #13. Experienced in small flashes near the during the battle in the ruins outside of Bazzoxan, in the following order:

Vision 1:

"Your vision goes dark, and for a moment you are under water with its weight pressing down, crushing the breath from your lungs, and a dim red light in the distance. Then, a moment later, you are back on the battlefield. But this is a different one than the one you left behind.

Your body is no longer your own, and you see the scene with a stranger's eyes. The sky is dim, and you are surrounded by creatures. You see your hands, before you holding a shield and a spear, and you are in the middle of a bloody, brutal battle. You swing around and stab your spear through a slavering demon with red glowing eyes, before spinning and slicing through the throat of a hooded human mage with unnaturally black eyes, and evil-looking tattoos. You feel disgust wash over you at this reminder that fellow humans would choose to align themselves with the Abyss.

Nearby, you see one of your allies is gored brutally by a boar-like beast with huge, black dripping tusks. You cry out, and spin and dodge toward them even though you know you are too late. You cut and stab, the smell of blood and rotten eggs burning your nose and the sickly pink light from the twin moons above illuminating the horrors around you. A desperate thought flashes clearly through your mind: "Where is she? Where is Perigee?""

Vision 2:

“Where is she? Where is Perigee?" With that thought lingering in your head, you have now moved deeper into the fray. Blood and black ichor make your hands slick, but your grip on your weapons is firm and resolute. A winged demon with dripping black feathers rakes its claws down your back. You barely feel it, noticing only long enough to deliver a precise spear strike cleanly through its skull. A thought flashes through your mind frantically: "I must find Perigee."

Deep in your subconscious, you feel a sense of deep fear and dread. If a being created and blessed by the Moon Weaver herself cannot overcome the curse of Ruidus, the evil of the Abyss, what other hope is there?

You put your head down and charge through a clump of lesser demons, shield first, sending them tumbling to the ground. You are dimly aware that they are being crushed under the hooves of the bull demon goring another of your allies, but are singularly focused on your goal of finding your ally.

A divinely bright blast of radiant light shocks the field off to your right, and you feel a brief moment of relief as you finally see Perigee in the distance, alive and fighting fiercely. Her white wings shine with brilliant moonlight as she expertly navigates the air above the battlefield, raining down unyielding, powerful blows on any enemy within reach of her gleaming mace. You feel a surge of warmth and confidence. Gifted to the material world by the Moonweaver herself, she is strong, she is unyielding. She can turn the tides, she can prevail against the curse. You know this; she has reassured and saved you countless times already.

You watch as she approaches the largest enemy on the field: a black-winged demon wielding a sword made of hellfire. He turns to her with a snarl, and they engage in a brutal duel. A red-hot whip snakes out from the beast's other hand, and wraps around Perigee's forearm. You wince, and can practically smell her burnt flesh from here, can almost feel the oppressive heat and searing pain. The beast yanks her closer, and her weapon meets his with an earth-shattering crash."

Vision 3:

"Locked in battle with the demon general, you hear Perigee begin chanting a prayer to the Moon Weaver, her words floating across the top of the battlefield as if projected. You see sparks fly as she deflects another fiery sword strike. You smell burning feathers and flesh. Her voice grows louder and louder, and the holy moonlight shining from her becomes even more impossibly bright; you have to avert your eyes, and you cry out her name blindly. Her words take on a sense of urgency, of desperation, and you feel growing horror as you sense what is coming.

You hear a great roar as the demon delivers another strike, and Perigee shouts out a final celestial phrase. Abyssal and divine fire clash, and one final cataclysmic strike of searing heat and blinding light explodes across the battlefield. Your own wordless scream is drowned out, and your vision briefly goes black.

As your vision returns, your ears ringing and eyes blinking away tears, you see a scene of devastation around you. Everyone who had the misfortune of being close to the final strike, friend and foe alike, has been reduced to ash and char, still smoldering on the ground. At the center of the blast radius, where the final strike fell, you see a new, sharp, glass-like growth juts from the ground sharply, as if the sheer heat and power of the blast crystallized into the ground around it. The demonic general, defeated, is already melting away into smoking, black ichor. On the battlefield around you, the enemy flees and scatters in fear, as their general lies dead and defeated.”

Vision 4:

“But this victory was won at a great cost. You look around frantically, and quickly realize that all that remains of Perigee–your loyal ally, your celestial guide, your friend–is a light rain of glowing ash, falling from the sky and settling gently around you like snow.

You fall to your knees. Time somehow slows and speeds at the same time. Your remaining allies regroup and raise a weary cheer, for they have survived another day, defeated a great demon, repelled another wave from the evil temple. But you cannot find joy, cannot even breathe for the sharp pain of loss in your chest.

Above you, through the gently-falling ash, the white moon Catha shines full and beautiful. Beside it, the red moon Ruidus gleams angry and hateful. You silently curse the red angry moon above you, as it has cursed you. You feel tears begin to run down your face, and call out a silent prayer to anyone who will listen."
