An Introduction to Exandria

  • Category The World

History Exandria

Before there were mortals there were the Primordials, elemental titans who shaped the raw stuff of the world into mountains and seas. Then came the gods, arriving from beyond the stars, and they were not content to leave the world as they found it. They remade it — and from their work rose the first peoples, who would one day call this world Exandria.

The gods did not all share one purpose. Those who loved their creations became the Prime Deities. Those who would rule or ruin them became the Betrayer Gods. Their disagreement could not last, and the world has been scarred by their wars ever since.

For an age, mortals flourished. In the Age of Arcanum, archmages raised cities into the clouds and bent the laws of magic to their will, growing so mighty that some came to rival the gods themselves. It is in this age that our story has its root, for it is the age that ended in the Calamity — and the age that made, and unmade, a hero named Alyxian.

The Drowned Prison

Beneath the Lucidian Ocean, between the continents of Wildemount and Marquet, the seafloor falls away into a trench so deep that no light has ever touched its bottom. Sailors call it the Netherdeep, and they do not sail above it if they can help it. Something is down there. Something that was a person, once.

That presence has begun to stir. Its grief seeps upward through the cold water and into the dreams of those who sleep near the coast — a voice, drowning, calling for help it cannot name. Where the dream falls, strange things follow: red tides, restless dead, and relics that will not stay buried.

The Stage of Our Campaign

Our tale begins far from any throne or tower, in the fishing town of Jigow on the Greying Wildlands of Xhorhas, where the Festival of Merit is about to begin. From there the call will draw the party across the sea to the glittering city of Ank'Harel in Marquet, and at last down into the black water itself.

"Everyone hears the sea. Not everyone hears something answer back."

We enter, now, into the deep history of the world — the war that broke it, and the hero whose pride and grief left a wound that has never healed.
